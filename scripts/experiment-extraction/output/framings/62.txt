NEE – New York Energy Storage Market Entry Decision Frame (2026)

# 1. Context Block

New Energy Equity (NEE) is evaluating entry into the New York energy storage market, with an emphasis on distributed solar + battery storage and standalone battery systems.

The initiative originates from internal efforts to expand beyond solar-only development and to integrate storage as a complementary capability. Current internal confidence in modeling and storage-specific economics is limited, and the company seeks external analytical rigor to validate assumptions and determine commercial viability.

New York has been identified as the immediate focus state due to:

- Established distributed solar activity
- Existing incentive structures
- Defined interconnection pathways
- Active market participation frameworks under NYISO
While the broader strategic ambition may include expansion beyond New York, this decision concerns only long-term viability within New York.

NEE maintains internal storage-related capability, including a dedicated interconnection specialist and a storage specialist, supporting queue navigation, interconnection analysis, and storage-specific modeling inputs.

The outcome of this work will inform a Board-level decision targeted for March 31, 2026.

# 2. Decision Block

Core Decision

Should NEE enter the New York energy storage market on a long-term basis?

The decision is binary:

- Go – Commit to sustained NY market participation.
- No-Go – Do not pursue NY storage market entry at this time.
All analysis must support answering this decision.

# 3. Key Questions

These questions must be explicitly answered to resolve the decision.

### BLOCKING QUESTIONS (Must Be Answered to Proceed)

- Do NY storage market economics (hybrid and/or standalone) meet NEE’s required financial thresholds under realistic assumptions?
- Is the NY policy, incentive, and regulatory environment sufficiently stable and durable to support long-term participation?
- Can projects be developed, interconnected, and monetized within acceptable execution timelines and risk parameters?
- Is there a credible buyer / IPP demand structure for projects under NEE’s non-ownership model?

### HIGH PRIORITY QUESTIONS (Material to Decision)

- Which configuration (standalone vs solar + storage) produces superior risk-adjusted economics in NY?
- What proportion of projected revenue is policy-driven versus merchant-exposed, and how volatile is the revenue stack?
- Are interconnection constraints likely to materially limit scalability?
- What is the realistic replicability of viable project archetypes within NY?

### MEDIUM PRIORITY QUESTIONS (Contextual but Not Blocking)

- What EMS, degradation, and augmentation risks materially affect long-term value?
- What operational or organizational adjustments are required to support NY storage participation?
All risks identified in prior materials have been reframed as neutral, answerable questions.

# 4. Success Criteria

If the decision is Go, success is defined as:

### Financial Outcomes

- Projects meet or exceed Board-defined return thresholds.
- Revenue profile is sufficiently stable relative to risk tolerance.
- Capital deployment produces acceptable risk-adjusted returns.

### Strategic Outcomes

- Establishes a credible and repeatable development pathway in NY.
- Demonstrates configuration(s) that are economically viable under NY market conditions.
- Builds internal competence in storage development and monetization.

### Operational Outcomes

- Projects can be executed within defined timeline constraints.
- Development and monetization processes are scalable.
Prioritization:

Financial viability is primary. Strategic positioning is secondary. Organizational build-out is tertiary.

No feasibility judgments are embedded here.

# 5. Assumptions

These are hypotheses that must hold true for long-term NY market entry to be viable. Each must be explicitly validated during analysis.

### 1. Battery Cost Competitiveness

[Client-stated input — cost modeling basis]

Installed battery system costs (including BOS, interconnection, integration, and augmentation) remain within the cost assumptions used in the financial model that are required to achieve Board-defined return thresholds.

### 2. Revenue Sufficiency

[Inferred — derived from core decision logic]

Under base-case modeling assumptions, the NY revenue stack (policy-driven incentives plus market-based dispatch revenues) produces project returns that meet Board-defined financial thresholds.

### 3. Merchant Exposure Tolerance

[Pending client confirmation — see Section 0, Item A3]

Achieving Board-defined financial thresholds does not require merchant revenue exposure beyond the client-confirmed risk tolerance.

### 4. Incentive Accessibility

[Client-stated reliance on ITC and NY incentives]

Federal ITC and relevant NY incentive programs are legally and structurally accessible to NEE’s develop-and-sell project model for projects reaching commercial operation within the evaluation period.

### 5. Policy Stability

[Inferred — durability requirement for long-term entry]

No foreseeable regulatory changes within the defined projection horizon materially eliminate or reduce the primary modeled revenue streams for NY storage projects.

### 6. IPP Buyer Demand

[Client-stated strategic positioning — IPP model]

There is sufficient market demand from IPPs for NY storage-inclusive projects at pricing levels consistent with the development margins assumed in the financial model.

### 7. Replicability

[Inferred — required for long-term market entry classification]

The economically viable project configuration identified through modeling can be deployed across multiple NY sites that meet defined technical and economic screening criteria.

### 8. Interconnection Feasibility

[Inferred — execution feasibility requirement]

Interconnection timelines and upgrade costs for NY storage projects remain within the assumptions used in the financial model and do not reduce modeled returns below Board-defined thresholds.

### 9. Configuration Viability

[Pending clarification — see Section 0, Item D10/D11]

At least one distributed-scale configuration (standalone or hybrid, ~5 MW class or optimized equivalent) can meet financial thresholds under NY conditions.

### 10. Organizational Capability

[Inferred — execution dependency]

NEE can resource the technical, financial, and operational capabilities required to develop and transact NY storage projects within the decision timeline and at costs consistent with modeled returns.

# 6. Constraints

Constraints are fixed boundaries.

### Decision Deadline

Board decision required by March 31, 2026.

### Geographic Scope

State of New York only.

### Ownership Model

NEE will develop projects for sale or partnership with IPPs and will not retain long-term asset ownership.

### Market Focus

Distributed / non-utility-scale configurations (exact size constraint subject to clarification per Section 0).

### Definition of Market Entry

For purposes of this decision, “market entry” refers to committing to sustained participation in the NY energy storage market and does not refer solely to conducting a limited pilot study without long-term commitment.

### Time Horizon

Projection horizon to be clarified (see Section 0).

No solutions may violate these constraints.

### Ownership Model

NEE will develop energy storage projects for sale to or partnership with IPPs and will not retain long-term asset ownership.

All financial modeling and strategic evaluation must assume a develop-and-sell structure rather than long-term balance sheet ownership.
